## Actionalbe Insights
- Audit your social environment now!
    - Write down the 5-8 people you spend the most time with. What values do they normalize? Competition? Consumption? Curiosity? Growth? Cynicism?
- Reduce status competition around you
    - Curate social media to remove constant lifestyle comparison
    - Stop performing success for others
    - When someone around you fails, make it safe, not shameful

## Ponder
- When something is going well or badly for you, ask: what invisible environmental factor might explain this that I am ignoring because I am too focused on individual behavior?

## Highlights

### The Roseto Mystery

Life was hard.

I rearly find anyone from Roseto under the age of 65 with heart disease.

The death rate from all causes in Roseto, in fact, was 30 to 35 percent lower than expected.

Whopping 41 percent of their calories came from fat. Nor was this a town where people
got up at dawn to do yoga and run a brisk six miles.

The secret of Roseto wasn't diet or exercise or genes or location. They picked up on the
particular egalitarian[^1] ethos of the community, which discouraged the wealthy from flaunting their
success and hehlped the unsuccessful obscure their failures.

Rosetans had created a powerful, protective social structure capable of 
insulating them from the pressures of the modern world.

We need to appreciate the idea that the values of the world we inhabit and the
people we surround ourselves with have a profound effect on who we are.
> Tight communities that produce health and equality can also suppress ambition, individuality, and innovation.
>
> Every system has costs. Roseto traded individual freedom for collective health. 
>
> Strong community existed alongside good health. But maybe both were caused by a third thing: low chronic stress from economic security, shared identity reducing existential anxiety, or simple lifestyle regularity. The community might have been a marker of something deeper, not the mechanism itself.


[^1]: Egalitarian refers to
a belief system or society holding that all people are fundamentally equal, possessing the same worth, rights, and opportunities.
